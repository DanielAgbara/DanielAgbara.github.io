Overview

As part of a commissioned design initiative by the CEO of Green Ventures Tanzania, I was tasked with developing six innovative chair designs for a new line of sustainable, ergonomic seating products. These chairs were intended to combine environmental responsibility, modern aesthetics, and evidence-based ergonomic support.

The designs drew on medical and biomechanical principles to reduce pressure on the pelvis and improve posture, especially during extended sitting sessions.

Ergonomic Foundation

This project was informed by research on seat tilt and discomfort caused by prolonged weight on the ischial bones. As shown in the reference material (image provided), pressure hotspots are created when sitting on a flat surface, restricting circulation and causing discomfort. To address this, several of the chair designs featured:

  • Subtle seat tilt to promote natural posture and redistribute weight
  • Contoured seating surfaces to avoid shearing forces and slippage
  • Integrated backrest angles that reduce pressure when reclining
  • Designs that enable reclining while preventing forward slide
