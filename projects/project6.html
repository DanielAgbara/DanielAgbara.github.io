Inclusive Merry-Go-Round for Children with Disabilities

Overview

As part of a collaborative design course, our multidisciplinary team set out to design an inclusive merry-go-round that enables children who use wheelchairs to participate safely and joyfully in playground activities. Guided by the principles of universal design, we created a solution that balances safety, accessibility, and fun.

Our mission was rooted in creating a shared play experience—where all children, regardless of ability, can engage equally. To accomplish this, we conducted stakeholder interviews, developed multiple concept prototypes, and evaluated them using a structured decision-making process. Our final concept integrates a rotating platform, wheelchair-compatible seating, safety features, and a reliable drive mechanism—meeting both technical specifications and community needs.

Mission Statement

We aimed to build a play structure that embodies inclusivity and celebrates diversity. The project’s mission focused on creating a safe, accessible, and engaging playground experience where all children could play and make memories together— especially those who often face barriers in traditional playgrounds.

Stakeholder Analysis

We identified and prioritized the following key stakeholder groups:

  • Children with Disabilities: The primary users; their safety, accessibility needs, and user experience were central.
  • Parents and Caregivers: Focused on ease of use, reliability, and comfort while ensuring child safety.
  • Local Community: Emphasized the broader social benefit, inclusion, and neighborhood impact.

Input was collected via interviews, surveys, and secondary research, helping shape the project’s design constraints and usability goals.

Target Specifications

Based on stakeholder feedback, the following features were defined as essential:

  • Rotating Platform: The central base enabling movement and engagement.
  • Wheelchair-Compatible Seating: Secure, flush-mounted spaces for easy wheelchair access without needing to transfer.
  • Safety Barriers and Handrails: Prevent falls and provide support.
  • Drive Mechanism: Ensures smooth, controlled rotation (manual or motorized, depending on final concept refinement).

Concept Generation

We used a structured process involving:

  • Market research and analysis of existing inclusive playground equipment.
  • Brainstorming sessions to generate creative and feasible solutions.
  • Feedback from experts and iterative refinement of ideas.

Concept Design Overview

Each of the three proposed concepts offered different layouts and drive solutions. Although the presentation does not detail each version visually, they likely included variations on:

  • Platform size and rotation method
  • Seating arrangement for children with and without mobility devices
  • Safety mechanisms (e.g., barrier style, seatbelts)
  • Potential manual vs. electric drive mechanisms

Concept Selection

A decision matrix was used to objectively compare the three concepts. Criteria included:

  • Safety
  • Accessibility
  • Durability
  • Cost-effectiveness
  • User enjoyment

The final design was selected for its balanced performance across all categories and strong alignment with both technical feasibility and stakeholder expectations.

Conclusion

This project was more than just a technical challenge—it was a mission to make play accessible. By centering the voices of children with disabilities and their communities, we created a thoughtful design that fosters inclusion, equity, and joy. The Merry-Go-Round prototype stands as a symbol of what engineering can achieve when guided by empathy, collaboration, and purpose.
