About Me

I am a Mechanical Engineer currently pursuing a Master’s degree at the University of Texas at Austin. I earned my Bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering with a minor in Computer Science from Drexel University — a combination driven by my deep interest in Robotics and Automation, particularly in the areas of Control Systems, Dynamics, and Reinforcement Learning.

Projects

Lane Detection and Control System

For my senior design project, I collaborated with the Zhou Robotics Lab and three other Drexel University students to develop a lane detection and control system that ensures a robot car maintains its lane in a mock city. This project was part of the lab’s Autonomous Navigation Testbed Development. Click for more information.

Frogger Reinforcement Learning Agent

Reinforcement Learning is a powerful approach for training autonomous systems through interaction with their environment. In this project, I implemented a Q-Learning algorithm to train an AI agent capable of successfully playing and winning the classic Frogger arcade game across varying difficulty levels — Easy, Medium, and Hard. Click for more information.

Planar 2R Robot Manipulator Simulation

Numerical Methods are great tools for Mechanical Engineering Design. This project involved developing a numerical simulation of a planar 2R robotic manipulator subjected to time-varying joint torques. The purpose was to model, solve, and visualize the dynamic response of the manipulator using multiple numerical integration techniques, while analyzing motion, energy, and power consumption over time. Click for more information.

Knight Trials

Knight Trials is a collaborative game development project created by a team of three students at Drexel University. Designed to challenge players’ strategy and reflexes, the game immerses them in a medieval fantasy world where they must prove themselves as worthy defenders of the Realm. Players journey through three uniquely designed elemental mazes—Earth, Ice, and Mud—each presenting distinct terrain mechanics, environmental hazards, and dynamic enemy encounters. Click for more information.

Closed-Loop Hydroponic Planter

As part of a senior design project for MEM 459, my team and I developed an automated hydroponic irrigation system using an Arduino-based closed-loop PID controller. The system dynamically maintains optimal moisture levels using real-time feedback and modular hardware integration. Click here for more information.

Inclusive Merry-Go-Round

As part of a collaborative design course, our team designed a wheelchair-accessible merry-go-round based on principles of universal design. This multidisciplinary effort aimed to provide inclusive playground access for children with disabilities by balancing safety, fun, and accessibility. Click here for more information.

Green Ventures Chair Design

As part of a commissioned design initiative by the CEO of Green Ventures Tanzania, I was tasked with developing six innovative chair designs for a new line of sustainable, ergonomic seating products. These chairs combined environmental responsibility, modern aesthetics, and evidence-based ergonomic support. Click here for more information.
